Carolyn Zhou

GoodCook

Challenge

To design a system and its visual voice to help college students build lasting habits of healthy eating.

Outcome

GoodCook is a program that promotes healthy eating through social interaction and peer accountability. We provide a style guide for the use of our visual components across our touch points.

Role

  • Designer
  • User research
  • Concept development
  • Visual development

Collaborators

  • Natalie Harmon
  • Christie Chong

Overview

Our system consists of 3 main touch points and guides users through the process of building healthy eating habits. A pop-up shop on campus raises awareness about GoodCook. It also provides bags containing vegetables, incentivizing students to cook and directing them to the website. There, users are encouraged to start tracking a long-term routine with friends who can take turns cooking for each other, both saving time (on non-cooking days) and gaining accountablility for their diet.

Spatial Piece: Pop-Up Shop

Print Piece: Bag

Digital Piece: Website

Research

Key Findings

For our audience, we decided on college students because there is an obvious need for healthier habits. We conducted interviews with our peers to find out about their eating patterns.

We were surprised to find that students avoid cooking mainly because they feel incompetent at it. We aimed to help people overcome their fear of cooking by presenting it as a simple and beneficial habit. We also realized that a social aspect to cooking correlated to better quality cooking, so our secondary goal was to encourage cooking in community.

I don't think I cook properly. I kind of just make whatever's easiest.

Development

Visual Exploration

Once we had our concept, we worked on forming a visual language that supported it. We tested many different colors and styles, asking people how each combination made them feel. We sought to create a visual voice that felt fun, playful, and exciting.

Shown below are both individual and combined explorations.

In the end we decided on an off-beat color palette and clean edges. We found that people thought the style and colors felt unique and quirky. Ultimately, the combination made for a memorable visual style, which is valuable for a brand. We also chose to use the Gotham font family for its round, friendly qualities.

Final Style Guide

This style guide outlines our research validation, the context in which each piece exists, and the relationship between written content and imagery in our system.

Learning Outcomes

  • Identifying a problem and its constraints, audience, and physical context
  • Developing a visual voice that complements a message and subject matter
  • Developing a cohesive visual system for different platforms and media
  • Discerning how to design calls to action for short-term prompts (pop-up shop & bag) and long-term tools (website)

Reflection

If I were to do this project again, I would revisit more dynamic and interesting ways to use our visual system across our different media. For example, the use of our background pattern is consistent but also static from print to digital. I would also like to think deeper about the partnerships and services that would support this program, such as local vendors and farmers who might provide produce to give away.
