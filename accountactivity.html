Enhancing mobile account pages

Challenge

Redesign account pages in PNC’s mobile app to incorporate new data and content.

Outcome

A newly designed interaction with banking tools in account pages, as well as improved visual hierarchy for all account pages.

RoleDesign lead

TeamProduct manager (1), Developer team

Overview

This project was part of a larger initiative to combine PNC's two mobile apps: one for “general” customers, and one for customers with Virtual Wallet, a special set of accounts and banking tools.

The PNC app provides basic account info for all PNC accounts, while the other supports only Virtual Wallet accounts and exclusive banking tools. To bring VW tools to all PNC customers, my team worked with the PNC Mobile team to incorporate the tools into their app.

My task was to redesign their pre-existing Account Activity experience, which would be important for transitioning VW customers smoothly into a different app, and for other customers to discover the new tools at their disposal.

Research

Initial Interviews

I sought to learn how people generally used Account Activity and banking tools. These were informal interviews with colleagues, friends and family.

People visited Account Activity to check the state of their money and then decide whether they need to take an action, like make a transfer or pay a bill. I realized it acted like a dashboard that displayed data and offered action items.

Target User

I defined a target user to help guide our direction (who benefits most from extra banking tools? what kind of customer are we appealing to?).

Profile

  • Age: 30-40
  • Middle class workers & families
  • Rents or owns a home
  • Tech-savvy

User Needs

  • Be financially responsible (i.e. pay bills)
  • Save for short- and long-term financial goals

This user is often busy and wants to bank on-the-go. He likes the Virtual Wallet tools, but he only downloaded the main PNC app and didn't want to download multiple apps for one bank.

If I could see all my accounts in one place, and still have the VW tools, I'd use them all the time.

Information Structure

Next I sought to learn about customers' mental models of their VW tools and accounts, in order to implement structure and hierarchy. I recruited 10 users for each task.

A dendogram of 10 card sorts revealed strong correlations between some features, especially account balance & transactions (80%) and the savings tools (90%).

8 out of 10 users left "Balances" and "Transactions" on the first screen in paper prototype tests, where I asked users to rearrange content to suit their own needs.

Overall, users associated spending tools with checking accounts, and investment tools with savings. Proactive sorting by users also revealed certain data they valued more, like account balance and transactions.

Design

Development

Moving into higher-fidelity mockups, I began testing navigation, UI elements, and interactions that would help users feel capable, efficient and delighted when banking.

Below are some ideas I explored and tested (conducted by a research team, testing for task completion, ease of use and visual appeal).

Exploration: Carousel

Task completion: 66%; Usability: 4.9/7; Appeal: 6.1/7

Pros

  • Organized by function/topic

Cons

  • Cannot compare data across cards in the same group, which is sometimes useful
I wouldn't think tools and this data go in the same section.

Exploration: Tabbed Pages

Task completion: 80%; Usability: 5.5/7; Appeal: 5.4/7

Pros

  • Less content below the fold

Cons

  • More tabs to account for shorter pages
  • Difficult to assign page names to match user expectations
There's a lot hidden...I'm having to hunt for things.

Exploration: Tool Bar

Task completion: 100%; Usability: 6.5/7; Appeal: 6.2/7 (tested with icon/dropdown version)

Pros

  • Gives quick visual snapshot of financial activity

Cons

  • Less data able to be presented at once
  • Users sometimes can't tell what is interactive

66% of users most preferred versions with the "tool bar" concept, across multiple A/B tests where users completed a set of tasks in one version and then were shown other options.

Everything I need is on one page. I could easily manage the account without leaving this screen.

Iconography

As the icon version was testing best, I began further developing the new tool icons. I matched them to existing icons in the app, but opted to add color (borrowed from VW brand guidelines) to add visual delight in addition to familiar branding.

Through feedback gathered from stakeholders and users, I learned what parts worked well and continued to refine them. Some highlights include the enlarged account balance, the tool bar component, and the single page layout.

Results

  • New visual page layout applied to account pages for 8.3 million users
  • Tool bar design introduced for 4.8 million Virtual Wallet accounts
  • Learning to navigate relationships between coexisting products
  • Learning to work with the constraints of pre-existing patterns
  • Improved ability to rapidly prototype with Sketch and InVision Studio
