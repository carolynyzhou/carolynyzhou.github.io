Redesigning mobile account pages

Challenge

Redesign account pages in PNC’s mobile app to incorporate new data and content.

Outcome

A new design pattern for interactive banking tools in account pages, as well as improved visual hierarchy for all account pages.

RoleDesign lead

TeamProduct manager (1), Developer team

Overview

This project was part of a larger initiative to combine PNC's two mobile apps: one for “general” customers, and one for customers with Virtual Wallet, a special set of accounts and banking tools.

The general app provides basic account info (i.e. account balance, transactions), while other supports Virtual Wallet-exclusive banking tools. As part of an initiative to bring VW features to all PNC customers, my team worked with the PNC Mobile team to incorporate them into their app.

My task was to redesign their pre-existing Account Activity experience, which would be important for helping transition existing customers smoothly into their app, and for new customers to discover the new tools at their disposal.

Research

Initial Interviews

I sought to learn how people generally use Account Activity and digital banking tools. These were informal interviews with colleagues, friends and family.

People viewed Account Activity as a go-to "hub" of banking activity, where they check what action they need to take with their money, if any. This led me to set navigation to management tools (particularly the incoming VW tools) as a primary goal of my redesign.

Target User

I defined a target user to help guide our direction (who benefits most from extra banking tools? what kind of customer are we appealing to?).

Profile

  • Age: 30-40
  • Middle class workers & families
  • Rents or owns a home
  • Tech-savvy

User Needs

  • Be financially responsible (i.e. pay bills)
  • Save for short- and long-term financial goals

This user is often busy and appreciates the ability to bank on-the-go. He likes the VW tools, but he only has the PNC app which does not support them, because he didn't know to download the Virtual Wallet app.

I'd use the Virtual Wallet tools all the time, except I have to go on my computer to do anything.

Information Structure

Next I sought to learn about customers' mental models of their VW tools and accounts, in order to implement structure and hierarchy. I recruited 10 users for each task.

A dendogram of 10 card sorts revealed strong correlations between some features, especially account balance & transactions (80%) and the savings tools (90%).

8 out of 10 users left "Balances" and "Transactions" on the first screen in paper prototype tests, where I asked users to rearrange content to suit their own needs.

Overall, users associated spending tools with checking accounts, and investment tools with savings. Proactive sorting by users also revealed certain data they valued more, like account balance and transactions.

Design

Development

Moving into higher-fidelity mockups, I began testing navigation, UI elements, and interactions. Some questions I sought to answer:

  • Is important content the most visible/discoverable?
  • What is the best way to present all the data (pagination vs. scroll? icons vs. widgets?)

Below are some ideas I explored and tested (usability tests conducted by a research team, factoring in task completion and user scores for ease of use and visual appeal for the "overall" score).

Exploration: Carousel

Overall usability: 5.9/7

Pros

  • Ties function in to organization

Cons

  • Some content may be out of sight, out of mind
  • Cannot compare data across cards in the same group, which is sometimes useful

Exploration: Tabbed Pages

Overall usability: 6.5/7

Pros

  • Shorter pages, less below the fold

Cons

  • A lot of content is out of sight under other tabs
  • Difficult to assign page names to match user expectations

Exploration: Tool Bar

Overall usability: 6.7/7 (tested icon & dropdown version)

Pros

  • Gives quick visual snapshot of financial activity

Cons

  • Less data able to be presented at once
  • Users sometimes can't tell what is interactive

12 out of 15 users preferred the "tool bar" layout over the others, in an A/B test where they completed all tasks in one version and then were shown the other versions.

Iconography

As the icon version was testing best, I began further developing the new tool icons. I matched them to existing icons in the app, but opted to add color (borrowed from VW brand guidelines) to add visual delight in addition to familiar branding.

Through feedback gathered from stakeholders and users, I learned what parts worked well and continued to refine them. Some highlights include the enlarged account balance, the tool bar component, and the single page layouts.

Results

  • New visual page layout applied to account pages for 8.3 million users
  • Tool bar design introduced for 4.8 million Virtual Wallet accounts
  • Learning to navigate relationships between coexisting products
  • Learning to work with the constraints of pre-existing patterns
  • Improved ability to rapidly prototype with Sketch and InVision Studio
