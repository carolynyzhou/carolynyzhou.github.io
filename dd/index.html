Drawing & Design | Carolyn Zhou

What's This?

I offer 2 lesson tracks: Drawing & Design. Both build on a foundation of drawing, before splitting into different applications of these skills.

Track 1: Drawing

Students will learn to see, frame, and capture the visual world. They may continue to hone their art in a variety of media, including pencil, charcoal, ink and paint.

Track 2: Design

Students will learn not only drawing, but also visual communication by studying other aspects of composition, color, hierarchy, and image-text relationships.

Who Am I?

I’m a UX/Visual Designer at an industry-leading tech company, with an extensive background in pen-and-paper media. I'm also a Bay Area native and a graduate of Carnegie Mellon University School of Design & HCII.

Distance Learning —

Note: Because classes are virtual, I don't expect students to be fully stocked on all art supplies (normally I could provide materials). We may limit the range of media for the time being.

Method/Approach —

Young students and beginners learn basic drawing skills through reference pictures, still life, and craft projects. I accomodate for skill level based on age and provide guidance.

Older and more experienced students, who are more comfortable with sketching and refining mistakes, study more complex shading and volume, human form, and compositions by renowned artists and designers. Students will be encouraged to undertake a combination of technical practice and creative exploration.

For those in the Design track, we may pick up other tools including Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop. I will assign projects based on the student's proficiency in these software.
